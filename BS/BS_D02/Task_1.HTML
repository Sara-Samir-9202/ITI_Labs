Antonio Gaudi

Antonio Gaudí was a visionary Catalan architect, renowned for his unique approach that blended architecture with nature. Born in 1852, he dedicated his life to designing buildings that harmonize with their surroundings.

Park Guell

Park Güell is one of Gaudí's most famous masterpieces and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors can stroll along the serpentine benches and explore the vibrant colonnaded pathways.

Casa Batllo

Casa Batlló is a landmark building on Passeig de Gràcia in Barcelona, redesigned by Gaudí. The interiors are equally imaginative, with light wells and sculpted ceilings.

Casa Mila

Casa Milà, also known as La Pedrera, is another iconic Gaudí masterpiece. The rooftop chimneys resemble abstract sculptures, while interior spaces use innovative solutions.
